Getting Started and Things to Know

Magic: the Gathering is a lot easier to learn than many people would have you believe although it is ture that to master the game will take some time and understanding of myriad complex mechanics and interactions. That's for later though, for now lets start with the basics.

So, how do we actually play Magic: The Gathering?

Imagine you're the grand battle commander (stupid voice and hat optional), and your creatures are your frontline. Any monsters you have on the table can defend you, taking damage in your stead. As well as creature cards, you'll also have an array of spells, artefacts, enchantments and more. Everything is powered by land, a coloured energy, or mana, if you will.

The aim of the game is to kill the other player(s). You each start with 20 health points, which are typically tracked using a 20-sided dice, though several other acceptable ways of tracking life total exist for example, companion apps and specially designed life counters.
